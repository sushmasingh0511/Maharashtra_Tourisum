Aga Khan Palace

 

The Aga Khan Palace was built by Sultan Muhammed Shah Aga Khan in Pune, India. Built in 1892, it is one of the biggest landmarks in Indian history.In 1969, Aga Khan Palace was donated to the Indian people by Aga Khan IV as a mark of respect to Gandhi and his philosophy.
Hours: Open today 9AM to 5.30PM

Khadakwasla Dam

 

Khadakwasla is a dam on the Mutha River 20 km from the city of Pune in Maharashtra, India.This dam is one of the main sources of water for Pune. A few kilometres up the road, lies Sinhagad Fort and the twin dams of Panshet and Varasgaon which mainly supply water for irrigation.

Lal Mahal

 

The Lal Mahal (Red Palace) of Pune is one of the most famous monuments located in Pune, India. In the year 1630, Shivaji's Father Shahaji Bhosale, established the Lal Mahal for his wife Jijabai and son.
Address: National Highway 4, Kasba Peth, Pune, Maharashtra.                                         Hours: Open today 9AM-1PM, 4PM-8PM

Shreemant Dagdusheth Halwai Ganapati Temple

 

Shreemant Dagdusheth Halwai Ganapati Temple in Pune is dedicated to the Hindu God Ganesh. The temple is popular in Maharashtra and is visited by thousands of pilgrims every year.Devotees of the temple include celebrities and Chief Ministers of Maharashtra who visit during the annual ten-day Ganeshotsav festival.
Address: Ganpati Bhavan, 250, Budhvar Peth, Pune, Maharashtra 411002

Pashan City

 

Pashan is suburb of Pune, India. It is located off the Mumbai-Bangalore national highway, by-passing Pune city. Pashan is bordered by Baner in north, Sus village on west, Bavdhan in south and Pune University in east. Pashan is mostly a residential suburb of Pune and large portions are occupied by various governmental and educational institutions.

Parvati Hill

 

The temple is the oldest heritage structure in Pune and was built during the rule of the Peshwa dynasty. It is the second highest point in Pune. The main temple, Devdeveshwara, is made of blackstone. It was completed under Balaji Baji Rao, in 1749. Other temples are dedicated to Vitthal and Rukmini, Vishnu, and Kartikeya.

Raja Dinkar Kelkal Museum

 

The Raja Dinkar Kelkar Museum is in Pune, Maharashtra, India. It contains the collection of Dr. Dinkar G. Kelkar (1896-1990), dedicated to the memory of his only son, Raja. The collection was started around 1920 and by 1960 it contained around 15,000 objects. In 1962, Dr. Kelkar handed his collection to the Department of Archaeology within the Government of Maharashtra.
Address: 1377-78, Off. Bajirao Road, Natu Baug, Shukrawar Peth, Pune, Maharashtra 411002  Hours: Open today 10AM to 5.30PM

ShaniwarWada

 

Shaniwarwada is an 18th-century fortification in the city of Pune in Maharashtra, India. Built in 1732, It was the seat of the Peshwa rulers of the Maratha Empire until 1818. Shaniwarwada was largely destroyed in 1828 by an unexplained fire, but the surviving structures are now maintained as a tourist site.Shaniwarwada has most popular five gates:Dilli Darwaza, Mastani Darwaja, Khidki Darwaja, Ganesh Darwaja, .Narayan Darwaja
Address:Shaniwar Peth, Pune, Maharashtra 411030                                                                               Hours: Open today 9AM-8PM

Sinhgad Fort

 

Sinhgad is a fortress located roughly 30 kilometres southwest of the city of Pune, India. Previously called Kondhana. Perched on an isolated cliff of the Bhuleswar range of the Sahyadri Mountains. , It was called 'Kondana' after the sage Kaundinya. The Kaundinyeshwar temple, the caves and the carvings indicate that this fort had probably been built two thousand years back.
